Capgemini

Driving Business Transformation and Innovation

Founder

Capgemini was founded by Serge Kampf in 1967 in Grenoble, France. Initially named 'Société pour la Gestion de l'Entreprise et le Traitement de l'Information' (Sogeti), it later became Capgemini and grew to be one of the world's leading IT service providers.

Company History

From its inception, Capgemini has been a global leader in consulting, technology services, and digital transformation. In the 1970s, Capgemini expanded internationally, establishing a strong presence in North America, Europe, and Asia. In 2000, it acquired Ernst & Young's consulting division, further cementing its position in the global IT services industry.

Latest Industry Position

As of 2024, Capgemini is a global leader in the IT services industry with a workforce of over 350,000 employees across 50 countries. It ranks as one of the top IT consulting companies in the world, specializing in cloud computing, AI, digital engineering, and cybersecurity. Capgemini continues to drive business innovation by leveraging cutting-edge technologies to deliver superior solutions to its clients.

Other Information

Capgemini works across multiple industries including financial services, telecommunications, manufacturing, and healthcare. Its commitment to sustainability and responsible business practices has earned it numerous awards, and it consistently ranks high in employee satisfaction and workplace diversity.
